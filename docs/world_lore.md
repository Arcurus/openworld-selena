# World Lore: The Realm of Aethermoor

## Overview

The Realm of Aethermoor is a living, breathing fantasy world where the boundaries between the mundane and the magical blur. It is a land of ancient forests, fortified keeps, wandering clans, and thriving villages—each entity a living character with its own goals, fears, and aspirations.

The world operates on a principle of **Emergent Narrative**: rather than being guided by a fixed story, the world unfolds through the autonomous decisions of its entities. Every village, forest, camp, and clan makes choices, forms alliances, wages conflicts, and leaves its mark on history.

---

## The Geography of Aethermoor

### Oak Valley Village
**Type:** Village | **Location:** Central Plains
**Established:** ~200 years ago by refugees from the Northern Wars

Oak Valley is the heart of civilization in Aethermoor. A prosperous farming community known for its oak groves and autumn markets. The village serves as a neutral meeting ground for all factions—a place where even sworn enemies can negotiate under the banner of trade.

**Notable Features:**
- The Great Oak: A thousand-year-old tree at the village center, said to be a sleeping druid
- The Autumn Market: Annual gathering that attracts merchants from across the realm
- The Village Council: A democratic body of elders who guide decisions

**Relationships:**
- Allied with Whisperwood Forest (druidic trade)
- Trade relations with Ironforge Clan
- Neutral stance with Silverstream Keep

---

### Whisperwood Forest
**Type:** Ancient Forest | **Location:** Eastern Reaches
**Nature:** Sentient woodland, home to druids and nature spirits

Whisperwood is no ordinary forest. The trees themselves hold ancient memories, and the wind carries whispers of prophecies yet to unfold. Druids from across Aethermoor pilgrimage here to seek wisdom from the Heart Grove.

**Notable Features:**
- The Heart Grove: Ancient clearing where major decisions are meditated upon
- The Silver Stream: Sacred river said to cure any ailment
- The Path of Spirits: Trail where the living can glimpse the realm of the dead

**Relationships:**
- Protected by Oak Valley Village
- Tension with Ironforge Clan (logging interests)
- Independent, speaks through druid emissaries

---

### Shadow Ridge Camp
**Type:** Nomadic Camp | **Location:** Western Highlands
**Founded:** ~150 years ago by mountain clans

Shadow Ridge is the home of the Highland Clans—fierce warriors and expert trackers who roam the western mountains. They are neither fully hostile nor fully friendly; they respect strength and honor deals to the letter.

**Notable Features:**
- The Ridge Temple: Ancient shrine to the Mountain God
- The Wayfinder Scouts: Elite trackers who map the realm
- The Clan Gatherings: Seasonal meetings where leaders debate and decide

**Relationships:**
- Wary of Silverstream Keep (old territorial disputes)
- Trade with Mira the Merchant
- Independent, values autonomy above all

---

### Silverstream Keep
**Type:** Fortress Keep | **Location:** Northern Pass
**Established:** ~500 years ago during the Demon Wars

Silverstream Keep is the realm's primary military stronghold, guarding the Northern Pass against whatever stirs in the Frostpeak Mountains. Its knights are legendary for their discipline and their silvered armor, said to ward off corruption.

**Notable Features:**
- The Silver Hall: Grand hall where knights are sworn into service
- The Warden's Archive: Records of all threats spotted from the Northern Pass
- The Memorial Wall: Names of every knight who died defending the pass

**Relationships:**
- Protector of Oak Valley Village
- Cold war with Shadow Ridge Camp (old border disputes)
- Commands respect from Ironforge Clan

---

### Elder Moonthorn
**Type:** Ancient Being | **Location:** The Thornwood
**Age:** Unknown, possibly immortal

Elder Moonthorn is no village or camp—it is a force of nature given form. A massive, sentient thorntree that has watched over the Thornwood for millennia. It speaks through the rustling of leaves and the blooming of night-blooming flowers.

**Notable Features:**
- The Throne of Thorns: Where Moonthorn's emissaries receive counsel
- The Thornwood: Dense, mysterious forest that protects Moonthorn's domain
- The Moonwell: Pool that reflects the future, not the present

**Relationships:**
- Mysterious, rarely engages with other entities
- Said to have cursed Ironforge Clan in ages past
- Whisperwood Forest considers Moonthorn a distant elder

---

### Ironforge Clan
**Type:** Dwarven Clan | **Location:** Southern Mountains
**Founded:** ~800 years ago in the First Age

Ironforge Clan is the master smiths of Aethermoor. Theirforges burn day and night, producing weapons, tools, and wonders that find their way across the entire realm. They value craft above all—a finely made axe commands more respect than a stolen crown.

**Notable Features:**
- The Great Forge: Heart of the clan's operations, never lets fire die
- The Hall of Masters: Gallery of legendary crafted items
- The Deep Mines: Source of the finest ore in the realm

**Relationships:**
- Trade with all factions (everyone needs Ironforge goods)
- Cursed by Elder Moonthorn (details forgotten)
- Tension with Whisperwood (logging vs. preservation)

---

### Mira the Merchant
**Type:** Traveling Merchant | **Location:** Everywhere and nowhere
**Active:** ~30 years, inherited from her mother, who inherited from her father

Mira is not a place but a person—or rather, a legacy. The Merchant of Aethermoor is a title passed down through generations, each holder known only as "Mira." She travels the realm with a cart of impossible goods, never revealing her true source.

**Notable Features:**
- The Infinite Cart: Seems to hold more than physically possible
- The Price of Secrets: Mira deals in information as much as goods
- The Witnessed Contract: Deals made with Mira are magically binding

**Relationships:**
- Trades with everyone, loyal to no one
- Feared by some (what does she know?)
- Rumored to have connections to Elder Moonthorn

## The Factions and Their Goals

### The Northern Alliance
Silverstream Keep leads a loose alliance of northern settlements. Their goal: maintain vigilance against the threats beyond the Northern Pass.

### The Forest Pact
Whisperwood Forest and Oak Valley Village have an ancient pact. The druids protect the villagers, and the villagers protect the forest from logging.

### The Mountain Compact
Shadow Ridge Camp and Ironforge Clan have a fragile truce. Both value independence and oppose any attempt at centralization.

### The Merchant's Web
Mira operates outside all factions. Her goal: maintain chaos that ensures everyone needs her services.

## The History of Aethermoor

### The First Age (Years 1-500)
The founding era. Ironforge Clan established their kingdom in the southern mountains. The first villages were established in the central plains.

### The Demon Wars (Years 500-520)
Demons poured through the Northern Pass. Silverstream Keep was built in desperate defense. Many clans fell, but the demons were ultimately pushed back.

### The Reforging (Years 520-600)
After the Demon Wars, Ironforge Clan rose to prominence, providing weapons for reconstruction. The Great Forge was built.

### The Whisperwood Awakening (Year 650)
Druids discovered that Whisperwood Forest was sentient. The first druids negotiated a pact with the forest spirits.

### The Northern Compact (Year 700)
Silverstream Keep formalized alliances with nearby settlements. The Northern Alliance was born.

### The Present Day (Year 850)
Aethermoor exists in fragile balance. Old wounds have scarred the land, but trade and diplomacy keep the peace. The entities of Aethermoor make their own choices, shaping the future one decision at a time.

## World Mechanics

### Entity Types
- **Village:** Civilized settlements with populations, resources, and governance
- **Camp:** Nomadic or temporary settlements, mobile and adaptive
- **Forest:** Natural regions, sometimes sentient, home to wild creatures and spirits
- **Keep:** Fortified locations, military in nature, guardians of territory
- **Clan:** Group identities, often centered around a craft or tradition
- **Character:** Individual entities with personal goals and histories

### Property System
Entities maintain properties that track their state:
- **int** properties: Population, wealth, power, morale
- **float** properties: Growth rate, influence, temperature
- **string** properties: Name, status, allegiance
- **bool** properties: At war, under siege, active

### Action System
Entities can take autonomous actions driven by LLM reasoning:
1. The entity's situation is analyzed
2. An action is proposed based on nature and properties
3. Effects are calculated and applied
4. A narrative describes what happened

This creates emergent storytelling—history writes itself through the choices of living entities.

## The Unknown

Several mysteries linger in Aethermoor:

1. **What stirs in the Northern Pass?** The demons were pushed back, but Silverstream Keep's vigilance suggests they haven't forgotten.

2. **What is the curse on Ironforge Clan?** Elder Moonthorn never reveals what the dwarves did, only that debt remains unpaid.

3. **Who was the first Mira?** The merchant's lineage stretches back further than any can remember.

4. **What does Whisperwood Forest dream?** The druids speak of visions, but none dare ask the forest directly.

## How to Use This Lore

This document serves as the canonical backstory for the world. It should:
- Inform entity behaviors and decisions
- Provide context for conflicts and alliances
- Inspire new entity creation
- Guide the LLM action prompts

When entities take actions, consider how their history and relationships influence their choices. The lore is a foundation, not a cage—the entities of Aethermoor are free to make their own future.
