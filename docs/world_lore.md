# World Lore: The Realm of Aethermoor

## Overview

The Realm of Aethermoor is a living, breathing fantasy world where the boundaries between the mundane and the magical blur. It is a land of ancient forests, fortified keeps, wandering clans, and thriving villages—each entity a living character with its own goals, fears, and aspirations.

The world operates on a principle of **Emergent Narrative**: rather than being guided by a fixed story, the world unfolds through the autonomous decisions of its entities. Every village, forest, camp, and clan makes choices, forms alliances, wages conflicts, and leaves its mark on history.

---

## ⚔️ THE SHADOW AWAKENING (Current World Event)

*Year 850 - The Present Day*

For centuries, scholars dismissed it as myth. The Scrolls of the First Age spoke of a darkness that would return when the realm forgot its vigilance. Now, the signs are undeniable.

**What We Know:**
- Shadows in the Northern Pass stretch longer than the mountains themselves
- Whispers travel through the Whisperwood at night—words in no known language
- Animals flee southward in unprecedented numbers
- The Moonwell at Elder Moonthorn's domain has begun reflecting something other than the future

**The Prophecy of the Shadow Crown:**
> *"When the Silver Warden's line falters, and the last forge-fire dims,*
> *the Shadow shall rise from the Crown of Kings,*
> *and only the blood of the Forgotten Heir shall seal the door."*

This prophecy has four lines. Only two have ever been deciphered. The rest are written in shadow-script that burns any mortal eyes that attempt to read it.

**How This Affects Entities:**
- **Silverstream Keep** is mobilizing, believing they are the "Silver Wardens"
- **Whisperwood Forest** has closed its borders to all but druids
- **Ironforge Clan** works day and night, racing to forge something before the darkness comes
- **Oak Valley Village** prepares as a refuge, though the Council debates whether they should evacuate
- **Shadow Ridge Camp** interprets the prophecy differently—they see opportunity
- **Mira the Merchant** has doubled her prices, yet her cart is emptier than ever

## 🐉 Legendary Entities

Beyond the mortal factions, ancient beings and legendary figures shape the fate of Aethermoor.

### Velora the Undying
**Type:** Legendary Hero | **Age:** 847 years
**Location:** Wanders the realm, drawn to conflict

Velora was the greatest knight of the Demon Wars, said to have held the Northern Pass alone for seven days while the army retreated. When death finally claimed her, she refused it—her dying oath to protect Aethermoor bound her soul to the mortal realm.

**Appearance:** A knight in corroded silver armor, face obscured by a helm that reflects no light. She leaves no shadow.

**Abilities:**
- Cannot die by conventional means
- Appears when the realm faces existential threat
- Can imbue mortals with fragments of her undying will

**Relationships:**
- Founder of Silverstream Keep's knightly order
- Cursed by the Weaver (reasons unknown)
- Seeks the "Forgotten Heir" mentioned in the prophecy
- Occasionally trades secrets with Mira

**Quest Hook:** She remembers things from before the Demon Wars that no one else knows—including who the "Forgotten Heir" truly is.

---

### Shadowmaw the Dragon
**Type:** Ancient Dragon | **Age:** Unknown (pre-First Age)
**Location:** Slumbering beneath the Frostpeak Mountains

Before Ironforge Clan claimed the southern mountains, Shadowmaw ruled these peaks. The dwarves built their forges in the shadow of her slumber, believing her sleep was eternal. They were wrong.

**Appearance:** A dragon of impossible size, scales like obsidian mirrors. Her sleep is not death—it is *waiting*.

**Abilities:**
- When awake: can bring true darkness (not shadow, but absence of light)
- Dreams leak into reality—prophecies and visions
- Her heartbeat can be felt as earthquakes

**Relationships:**
- Ironforge Clan built their greatest forge directly above her heart to harness her warmth
- The source of the "curse" on Ironforge Clan—they took too much of her warmth
- Silverstream Keep watches her mountain more than the pass
- Elder Moonthorn and Shadowmaw have an ancient pact, details unknown

**Quest Hook:** Ironforge Clan has mined so deep they risk waking her. If she stirs, even Velora cannot stop her.

---

### The Crown of Shadows
**Type:** Cursed Artifact | **Created:** Year 1
**Location:** Unknown (possibly hidden, possibly worn)

The Crown was forged in the moment of creation itself—a byproduct of the gods' war that created Aethermoor. It grants the wearer power over shadows and the ability to see truth, but the cost is gradual erosion of self.

**Abilities:**
- Grants visions of possible futures
- Allows command of shadows and darkness
- Reveals hidden truths

**Cost:**
- Each use takes a memory
- After enough uses, the wearer becomes a shadow themselves

**Known Wearers:**
- King Valdris the First (Year 50-75) — vanished, became the first Shadow
- The Nameless Sage (Year 400) — used it to write the Prophecy, then destroyed themselves
- Unknown current bearer

**Quest Hook:** The prophecy mentions "the Crown of Kings"—is this the same artifact? Who wears it now?

---

### Sage of the Silver Stream
**Type:** Mysterious Figure | **True Age:** Unknown
**Location:** Appears near water, especially the Silver Stream

The Sage appears as an elderly figure wrapped in robes that seem woven from the stream's own water. They speak in riddles and answer questions with questions. Some say they are a spirit. Others say they were once mortal and the stream granted them immortality.

**Abilities:**
- Can see all possible futures (limited by the Moonwell's visions)
- Knows secrets from before the Demon Wars
- Cannot directly intervene, only guide

**Relationships:**
- Whisperwood druids consider them a saint
- Mira trades information with them
- Elder Moonthorn and the Sage share knowledge across the ages

**Quest Hook:** The Sage knows who the "Forgotten Heir" is, but will only reveal it to one who asks the right question.

---

### The Weaver
**Type:** Unknown | **Existence:** Disputed
**Location:** Everywhere and nowhere

The Weaver is either a myth, a god, or something else entirely. They are said to manipulate fate itself—not controlling outcomes, but adjusting the threads of causality so that certain events become more or less likely.

**Evidence of Existence:**
- Velora's curse came from "the one who tangles threads"
- Prophecies sometimes mention a "guiding hand"
- Certain unlikely events seem too convenient to be chance

**Abilities:**
- Can nudge probabilities
- Leaves "signs" for those who know how to read them
- Cannot create, only weave

**Relationships:**
- Cursed Velora (reasons unclear)
- Has dealings with Mira's lineage
- May have created the Crown of Shadows as a tool

**Quest Hook:** Is the Weaver real? Are they helping or hindering? Are they the true enemy—or the true ally?

## 🔗 Entity Relationships & Bloodlines

The entities of Aethermoor are not isolated—they are bound by history, marriage, debt, and fate.

### The Silver Warden Bloodline
**Current Head:** Commander Aldric of Silverstream Keep

The wardens trace their lineage to the hero who held the Northern Pass. This bloodline is said to carry a fragment of the light that drove back the demons.

**Connections:**
- Intermarried with Oak Valley Village founders (some villagers claim distant noble blood)
- Wardship oath binds them to protect the realm
- Velora the Undying watches this bloodline with particular interest

**Mystery:** The prophecy mentions "when the Silver Warden's line falters"—what constitutes faltering? Death? Dishonor? Loss of purpose?

---

### The Deep Mines Debt
**Owed by:** Ironforge Clan | **To:** Unknown (possibly Elder Moonthorn)

800 years ago, Ironforge Clan struck a deal to mine beneath the mountains. They found something—or took something. The debt they owe is written in shadow-script that even their dwarven scholars cannot read.

**Known Elements:**
- The mining was deeper than any have gone before
- Something was awakened—Shadowmaw began dreaming
- The Great Forge was built as appeasement, burning eternally

**Mystery:** What did Ironforge Clan take from the deep? Why does Elder Moonthorn never speak of it?

---

### The Refugee Heritage
**Origin:** The Northern Kingdoms (destroyed Year 600)

Oak Valley Village was founded by refugees from the Northern Kingdoms—lands that were consumed by shadow centuries ago. These refugees carried little, but some say they carried something else: a bloodline.

**Mystery:** The Northern Kingdoms were ruled by the Valdris dynasty. King Valdris wore the Crown of Shadows. If the refugees included even one of his blood... they might be the "Forgotten Heir."

---

### The Merchant's Web
**Founded by:** The First Mira

The merchant lineage is ancient—older than the Demon Wars, older than Ironforge Clan. Mira's predecessors predate writing in Aethermoor.

**Known Connections:**
- Mira's contracts are magically binding (where did this power come from?)
- Elder Moonthorn speaks to each Mira once, at the start of their tenure
- The Sage of the Silver Stream trades freely with Mira

**Mystery:** Why does the same name persist across millennia? Is Mira one person, many people, or something else entirely?

## 🔮 Mysteries & Prophecies

### The Prophecy of the Shadow Crown
Four lines, only two deciphered:
1. *"When the Silver Warden's line falters..."*
2. *"...and the last forge-fire dims..."*
3. (Unknown - burns mortal eyes)
4. *"...and only the blood of the Forgotten Heir shall seal the door."*

**Questions:**
- What causes a forge-fire to "dim"? Literal fire? Ironforge Clan's fall?
- Who or what is the "Forgotten Heir"?
- What is being sealed?

---

### The Lost Heir
The Northern Kingdoms fell, but royal bloodlines rarely die completely. The refugees who founded Oak Valley carried artifacts, secrets—and possibly heirs.

**Clues:**
- Some Oak Valley families have traditions they cannot explain
- The Great Oak was planted by a refugee who "remembered a crown"
- Mira's first trade with Oak Valley involved asking about family histories

**Quest Hook:** If the Forgotten Heir exists, they don't know it themselves. Finding them—and making them aware—would change everything.

---

### What Elder Moonthorn Witnessed
Elder Moonthorn is the oldest being in Aethermoor. They have watched since before the First Age. They remember things that have been forgotten.

**What We Know:**
- Moonthorn's curse on Ironforge Clan was not punishment—it was *warning*
- The Moonwell shows the future, but Moonthorn has seen past the future
- Moonthorn refuses to speak of what they saw in the year 800 (50 years ago)

**Mystery:** What did Moonthorn see in Year 800? Why did they begin preparing the Thornwood for something?

---

### The Shadow That Was
During the Demon Wars, something came through the Northern Pass that was not quite demon, not quite mortal. It was defeated, but not destroyed.

**Known:**
- It wore armor that moved like shadow
- It spoke with a voice that listeners described as "familiar"
- It was drawn to the Crown of Shadows

**Mystery:** What was it? Did it leave something behind? Is it waiting?

## 🗺️ Discovery System

When entities take actions, they might discover things about the world. These discoveries shape future possibilities.

### Types of Discoveries

**Artifacts:** Physical objects with power or significance
- Ancient weapons from the Demon Wars
- Fragments of forgotten magic
- Items belonging to legendary figures

**Knowledge:** Information that was lost or hidden
- History that contradicts accepted truth
- Secrets about entities or relationships
- Fragments of prophecy

**Locations:** Places that were hidden or forgotten
- Passages beneath known locations
- Hidden shrines or caches
- Paths between places that shouldn't exist

**Connections:** Relationships between things that weren't known to be connected
- Bloodlines that intertwine
- Events that influenced each other
- Entities that share origins

### Discovery Examples

| Entity Takes Action | Discovery Made |
|---------------------|----------------|
| Silverstream Keep sends scouts north | Find remnants of a camp—recent, marked with shadow-script |
| Whisperwood druids meditate on the whispers | Receive a fragment of the prophecy's third line |
| Ironforge Clan mines deeper | Uncover a chamber with dragon scales and dwarven bones |
| Mira takes a new route through Thornwood | Finds an old road, overgrown, leading somewhere impossible |
| Oak Valley sends traders to Silverstream | Traders notice Commander Aldric wears a pendant his father never had |

## The Factions and Their Goals

### The Northern Alliance
Silverstream Keep leads a loose alliance of northern settlements.
- **Goal:** Maintain vigilance against the threats beyond the Northern Pass
- **Current Focus:** Mobilizing for war that may already be here
- **Leaders:** Commander Aldric, Knight-Captain Seris

### The Forest Pact
Whisperwood Forest and Oak Valley Village have an ancient pact.
- **Goal:** Preserve the natural order and protect the realm's heart
- **Current Focus:** Sealing the forest and preparing evacuation routes
- **Leaders:** Archdruid Thaelon, Elder Merin of the Village Council

### The Mountain Compact
Shadow Ridge Camp and Ironforge Clan have a fragile truce.
- **Goal:** Maintain independence and strength
- **Current Focus:** Ironforge Clan races to forge something; Shadow Ridge watches
- **Leaders:** High Chieftain Bromund, Forge-Master Dralrik

### The Merchant's Web
Mira operates outside all factions.
- **Goal:** Maintain chaos that ensures everyone needs her services
- **Current Focus:** Gathering information on the Shadow's return
- **Leaders:** Mira (current)

### The Undying Oath
Velora the Undying works alone, bound by oath.
- **Goal:** Find the Forgotten Heir before the Shadow consumes all
- **Current Focus:** Watching the bloodlines of the realm
- **Leaders:** Velora herself

## The History of Aethermoor

### The First Age (Years 1-500)
The founding era. Ironforge Clan established their kingdom in the southern mountains. The first villages were established in the central plains. The Crown of Shadows was forged in the chaos of creation.

### The Demon Wars (Years 500-520)
Demons poured through the Northern Pass. Silverstream Keep was built in desperate defense. Velora held the pass alone. Many clans fell, but the demons were ultimately pushed back.

**What was lost:** The Northern Kingdoms, the First Writing, most knowledge of what came before Year 1.

### The Reforging (Years 520-600)
After the Demon Wars, Ironforge Clan rose to prominence, providing weapons for reconstruction. The Great Forge was built. The refugees of the Northern Kingdoms settled in what would become Oak Valley.

**What was found:** The Scrolls of the First Age (partial), fragments of the Prophecy.

### The Whisperwood Awakening (Year 650)
Druids discovered that Whisperwood Forest was sentient. The first druids negotiated a pact with the forest spirits. The Forest Pact was born.

### The Northern Compact (Year 700)
Silverstream Keep formalized alliances with nearby settlements. The Northern Alliance was born. Commander Aldric's grandfather became the first official Warden.

### The Shifting (Year 800)
Elder Moonthorn closed the Thornwood's borders without explanation. Ironforge Clan began mining deeper. Mira raised her prices for the first time in a century.

**Something changed. The realm felt it.**

### The Present Day (Year 850)
The Shadow Awakens. Aethermoor stands at the precipice. The entities of Aethermoor make their choices—ally or flee, fight or bargain, seek truth or hide from it.

**History is writing itself. Again.**

## World Mechanics

### Entity Types
- **Village:** Civilized settlements with populations, resources, and governance
- **Camp:** Nomadic or temporary settlements, mobile and adaptive
- **Forest:** Natural regions, sometimes sentient, home to wild creatures and spirits
- **Keep:** Fortified locations, military in nature, guardians of territory
- **Clan:** Group identities, often centered around a craft or tradition
- **Character:** Individual entities with personal goals and histories
- **Legendary:** Ancient beings, heroes, dragons, artifacts—entities of immense power or significance

### Property System
Entities maintain properties that track their state:
- **int** properties: Population, wealth, power, morale
- **float** properties: Growth rate, influence, temperature
- **string** properties: Name, status, allegiance
- **bool** properties: At war, under siege, active

### Action System
Entities can take autonomous actions driven by LLM reasoning:
1. The entity's situation is analyzed
2. An action is proposed based on nature, properties, and current events
3. Effects are calculated and applied
4. A narrative describes what happened
5. (Optional) A discovery is made—a connection, a fragment, a truth

This creates emergent storytelling—history writes itself through the choices of living entities.

### World Event Influence
When the Shadow Awakens is active, all entity actions have additional context:
- **Silverstream Keep:** More militaristic, distrustful, sees threats everywhere
- **Ironforge Clan:** Urgent, working against time, making deals they might regret
- **Oak Valley Village:** Divided—some want to flee, others to fight, others to hide
- **Whisperwood Forest:** Protective of knowledge, sharing fragments of prophecy
- **Shadow Ridge Camp:** Opportunistic, waiting to see who falls so they can rise
- **Mira:** Information-hungry, trading in secrets about the Shadow

### Activating World Events (Game Master Guide)

World events are the canonical way to introduce major story shifts that affect all entity behaviors. The WorldEvent system is integrated into the LLM action prompts, so when a world event is active, the LLM considers its influence when generating entity actions.

**To activate a world event:**

1. **Use the API** to add the world event:
   ```bash
   curl -X POST /api/world/events \
     -H "Content-Type: application/json" \
     -d '{
       "name": "The Shadow Awakens",
       "description": "Ancient darkness stirs in the north. Shadows stretch longer, whispers fill the Whisperwood, and animals flee southward.",
       "influence": "All entities become more paranoid and militaristic. Silverstream Keep is on high alert. Ironforge Clan rushes to complete their secret forge before darkness falls.",
       "active": true
     }'
   ```

2. **Monitor the effects** - Watch as entities react to the world event through their actions

3. **Deactivate or replace** world events as the story evolves:
   ```bash
   # Deactivate
   curl -X PUT /api/world/events/:id -d '{"active": false}'
   
   # Update influence as the event progresses
   curl -X PUT /api/world/events/:id -d '{"influence": "The darkness has arrived. All factions are in crisis mode."}'
   ```

**Key principle:** The `influence` field describes how entities should behave differently because of the world event. This influence is included in every LLM action prompt, ensuring consistent world-wide narrative effects.

## How to Use This Lore

This document serves as the canonical backstory for the world. It should:
- Inform entity behaviors and decisions
- Provide context for conflicts and alliances
- Inspire new entity creation
- Guide the LLM action prompts
- Enable meaningful discoveries

When entities take actions, consider:
1. How does the Shadow Awakens affect their decision?
2. What might they discover?
3. How do their relationships to legendary entities influence them?
4. What mysteries might they uncover?

The lore is a foundation, not a cage—the entities of Aethermoor are free to make their own future.

But the Shadow is waiting. And it has all the time in the world.
