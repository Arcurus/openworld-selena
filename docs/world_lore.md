### Ironforge Clan

**Type:** Faction
 | **Location:** (420, 350)

*Dwarven forges that woke a dragon, and now race to put it back to sleep.*

The Ironforge Clan has held the southern mountains for nine centuries. Their forges are older than the realm's first maps; their patron-kings carved the treaties that bound the Northern Alliance, the Forest Pact, and the Mountain Compact in iron and oath. They are the realm's armorers, its engineers, its memory.

Three centuries ago, they dug the deep shaft — the one that drops past the stone where the world is supposed to end. They found warmth below. They used it. The Great Forge still burns with heat that was never theirs to take, and in the chamber beneath it, something that was sleeping began to dream. Vaelthrix the Endless. The dragon they did not name, because naming her would be a kind of ownership, and they are not ready to own what they woke.

Today the Ironforge foremen will not go below the seventh level. Three of them have come back changed. One did not come back at all.

**Relationships:** Allied with Silverstream Keep and The Ashen Brothers. Rival of The Crimson Vein. Built the Great Forge above Vaelthrix's heart. Owes a debt to Elder Moonthorn that no one will speak of.

**Secrets:** The seventh-level foreman — the one who came back — keeps a journal. It is written in shadow-script, the same script as the Prophecy of the Shadow Crown. The journal's last legible entry, decoded by Whispering Court scholars, reads: *she is not angry; she is curious, and curiosity is older than anger.* The Ironforge Council has not told the rest of the realm. They are still deciding what to do.

### Keepers of the Eternal Flame

**Type:** Faction
 | **Location:** (400, 250)

*An order that tends a fire which has never gone out, and has never been explained.*

The Keepers of the Eternal Flame have held The Ashen Spire since before the Demon Wars. Their fire is not a forge-fire and not a beacon. It does not consume its fuel. It does not flicker in the wind. It burns the same color it has burned for a thousand years — a deep, almost-luminous ash-grey that scholars have not been able to name.

The order has thirteen members at any time. They do not recruit; they wait, and the fire chooses. The chosen arrive at the Spire alone, in the same year, between the autumnal equinox and the first frost. They do not know how they were chosen, only that they were, and that they cannot leave.

The Ashen Brothers — their cousins — were an offshoot, centuries ago, who wanted to *understand* the fire. The Keepers believe understanding it would extinguish it. The Brothers believe it would save them. Neither has been right yet.

**Relationships:** Allied with The Ashen Brothers. Tied to The Ashen Spire. Watched, in silence, by The Whispering Court. The Silverstream Keep's library holds three of their journals, all of them redacted.

**Secrets:** The fire's color is the same color as the light that pools in the deepest chamber of the Ironforge mines, beneath the Great Forge. No one has connected these two facts. The Keepers have. They have said nothing.

### The Ashen Brothers

**Type:** Faction
 | **Location:** (670, 80)

*A splinter order who left the Eternal Flame to study it, and have not been forgiven.*

The Ashen Brothers broke from the Keepers in the year 642, four years after the Demon Wars ended. Their heresy was simple: they believed the Eternal Flame was not sacred, it was *mechanism* — a deliberately maintained artifact, and one which could be replicated, even improved. They left the Spire with seven journals, a fragment of the flame's housing, and a vow to understand what the Keepers would only tend.

They built their chapter-house on the far side of The Ashen Spire, in a cave they call the Antechamber. They are still studying. They have not replicated the flame. They have, however, discovered that the flame is connected to something *else* in the mountain — a deeper heat, a deeper silence — and that whatever sleeps below, the flame is its clock.

The Keepers of the Eternal Flame no longer speak to them. The Ashen Brothers still leave a seat empty at their table, in case.

**Relationships:** Former allies of the Keepers of the Eternal Flame. Now estranged. Tied to The Ashen Spire (the Antechamber side). Have been seen meeting with The Hollow Hand under cover of night, and deny it.

**Secrets:** The Brothers have been trying to wake the Ashen Spire's fire with *different* fuel — the ash of a creature that died screaming, an alloy they will not name, a single tear from a willing Hollow Hand. The last attempt, three winters ago, made the fire burn *backwards* for six seconds. They have not tried again. They have not stopped trying.

### The Crimson Vein

**Type:** Faction
 | **Location:** (650, 380)

*A miners' guild that has been digging in the wrong direction for the right reasons.*

The Crimson Vein began as a Brackenhold Mine accident — a hundred miners trapped for nine days by a collapse that should have killed them all, and the dwarf who dug them out, Veska Redvein, who then refused to leave the deep even when the rest of her crew begged her. She came back up eventually, but she was not the same, and the deep was not the same.

The Vein is small — three hundred members, scattered across a dozen shafts — but it is the only faction that goes *down* by choice. They believe there is a vein of red metal that runs through the bones of the world itself, and that whoever finds it will own the next age. The Ironforge Clan thinks this is fairy-talk. The Vein thinks the Ironforge are afraid.

They are both right.

**Relationships:** Direct rival of Ironforge Clan (over the same mountains, opposite methods). Member presence in The Howling Ravine and Brackenhold Mine. A few Vein-walkers have been seen drinking with The Black Sail Reavers, which makes the Ironforge nervous for reasons they will not articulate.

**Secrets:** Veska Redvein, the founder, is still alive. She is one hundred and forty-three years old. She should not be. The Vein knows this. The Vein does not speak of it. The Ironforge Clan, recently, has begun to notice.

### The Bridge Guild

**Type:** Faction
 | **Location:** (380, 240)

*The civil engineers of the realm — and the only faction that has never been invaded, because invading them would mean losing the bridges.*

The Bridge Guild is, on paper, a trade guild. In practice, it is the realm's infrastructure ministry, its postal service, and its most reliable spy network. The Guild builds the bridges, maintains the roads, runs the ferries, and — quietly — keeps the realm connected. They have built every working bridge in the western half of the continent, including The Stonebridge, which is older than the Kingdom that used to be here.

The Guild is famously neutral. They will work for any faction that pays, and their contracts are magically binding in a way no other guild's are. They are also famously good at keeping secrets, because their chief surveyor, Hallan Drift, is half-blooded Whispering Court and can taste a lie from three rooms away.

The Free Caravan is their closest working partner. The Silverstream Keep's knights cross their bridges for free, in exchange for armed escort. The Black Sail Reavers have tried three times to burn The Stonebridge and have failed three times; the Guild does not say how.

**Relationships:** Tied to The Stonebridge, Crossroads Market, and the roads between Silverstream Keep and The Free Caravan's main routes. Long partnership with The Free Caravan. The Silverstream Keep's garrison crosses free; the Black Sail Reavers' raiding parties find the bridges mysteriously out of service.

**Secrets:** The Guild has been quietly building a *second* Stonebridge, on a route that does not exist on any current map. They have been doing this for forty years. They will not say what is on the other side. Hallan Drift has been seen in The Old Standing Stones, alone, at midnight, three times this year.

### The Free Caravan

The Free Caravan is a roving merchant convoy, three hundred wagons strong, that crosses the realm in a cycle that takes a year and a day. They sell everything and refuse to sell some things, and the refusals are the most reliable map of the realm's hidden economies that anyone has.

The Silver Order is the realm's largest and oldest knightly order, founded the year after the Demon Wars ended by Velora the Undying herself. They hold Silverstream Keep, the Northern Pass, and the oaths of every noble house in the northern half of the continent. They are the line in the sand.

**Secrets:** The Order's library contains a sealed letter from the *previous* Commander, dated the year of his death, addressed to Kira. Kira read it the day he died. ### The Thornwatch Rangers

**Type:** Faction

*Forest rangers who have never lost a tracker, never broken a vow, and never explained either.*

The Thornwatch Rangers are the realm's deep-wood specialists — scouts, trackers, hunters, and quiet deaths. They hold Shadow Ridge Camp as their base, but their territory is every forest, fen, and shadowed place between the mountains and the sea. They answer to no faction but the trees themselves, which the older Rangers claim is a sentence with a meaning.

The Rangers are small in number — never more than fifty, by their own oath — and they are recruited the old way: a child is found alone in the wood, three days' walk from anywhere, and the child is asked if they want to come home. Most do. The ones who do not are the ones who become Rangers.

Their bond with The Silver Order is a strange one. Kira Dawnblade was raised in the Rangers before she took the Silver oath; she is one of the few people in either organization who is trusted by both. **Secrets:** The Rangers have a *second* camp, deeper in the woods than Shadow Ridge, that is not on any map. They take their dying there. They take their oaths there. They take their most-wanted criminals there. The camp is real; its location is not a secret so much as a fact that the wood itself seems to have agreed not to remember.

### The Whispering Court

**Type:** Faction
 | **Location:** (660, 50)

*A court that meets in the old stones, and whose judgments are not sentences but *fates*.*

The Whispering Court is the oldest continuous institution in the realm. Its judges are not elected, appointed, or born; they *arrive*, one at a time, in years when the Standing Stones are said to whisper loudest. There have been seven judges in living memory, and the Court's records go back nine centuries.

The Court does not enforce law. It issues *judgments* — formal, binding, sometimes paradoxical statements about what is to be done, who is to do it, and what is to follow if it is not. The realm's kings once obeyed these judgments as a matter of course. The realm's current powers obey them as a matter of prudence.

The Court's meetings are held at The Old Standing Stones, on nights when the moon is dark and the wind is from the east. No one outside the Court knows what is said there. The Wandering Bard claims to have heard, and will sing one verse of it for a single gold piece; the verse is different every time, and never quite fits the rest of the song.

**Relationships:** Holds court at The Old Standing Stones. The Keepers of the Eternal Flame watch the Court in silence, and the Court watches them back. Mira the Merchant has done business with the Court exactly once, and will not say what she traded. Zephyrus the Oracle is rumored to be the Court's unofficial weather-reader.

**Secrets:** The Court's seventh judge, who arrived seven years ago, has not been seen outside the Standing Stones since. The Court's sixth judge, who should have died, is also not dead. The Wandering Bard knows which is which. The Wandering Bard will not say.

### The Whispering Roots

**Type:** Faction
 | **Location:** (100, 410)

*Druids of the deep wood, whose oldest member once traded a name with the wind itself.*

The Whispering Roots are the druid circle of the Whisperwood Forest — the largest and oldest of the realm's three druidic orders, and the one that has refused, three times, to be drawn into the political map. They hold the pact with the forest itself, signed (they claim) before the Ironforge Clan existed.

Their leader, in the formal sense, is Elder Moonthorn — but Moonthorn has not been seen in decades, and the day-to-day voice of the circle is Zephyrus the Oracle. Zephyrus is not a druid in the formal sense; he is a *listener* — a man who, in the year of his naming-day, walked into the wood and came back speaking a language that the wind understood. He has been the Roots' voice for forty years.

The Roots are the only faction in the realm that has open, ongoing dialogue with Vaelthrix the Endless. They claim this is a courtesy, not an alliance. The Silverstream Keep garrison is not convinced. Neither is The Hollow Hand.

**Relationships:** Tied to Whisperwood Forest, Willowfen Marsh, and The Silverleaf Sanctuary. Allied with The Circle of the Green and The Thornwatch Rangers. The Hollow Hand has tried, twice, to corrupt the Roots' younger members; both attempts failed, and the Roots have not said how.

**Secrets:** Zephyrus the Oracle has been receiving *dreams* from Vaelthrix the Endless for the past three years. He has shared them with no one, not even Elder Moonthorn. The dreams are about a door. The door is in The Drowned City. The door has been closed for a thousand years, and it is, the dreams insist, *not opening yet*.

### The Circle of the Green

**Type:** Faction
 | **Location:** (110, 240)

*A druid circle that tends the small green things — the gardens, the farms, the children who come home to them.*

The Circle of the Green is the realm's smallest druidic order, and the one that has stayed closest to mortal life. Where the Whispering Roots tend the deep wood, the Circle tends the edges: the hedgerows, the kitchen gardens, the village greens, the small still pools where children leave dandelion-floats on the solstice.

The Circle's seat is The Silverleaf Sanctuary, a hidden grove at the edge of the Whisperwood. From there, they look after Greenmark Farm, Hollowmere Village's herb gardens, and the children's dreams of the northern villages. They are the druids the villagers *know* — the ones who come when the livestock are sick, when the milk turns, when a child will not wake up.

The Circle is led, in practice, by no one. They vote on every decision, and the vote always passes unanimously, because the oldest member — who is also the youngest member, in a way no one will explain — always says the same thing at the end of every vote: *we will see*.

**Relationships:** Tied to The Silverleaf Sanctuary, Greenmark Farm, Hollowmere Village, and the smaller villages in the eastern wood. Allied with The Whispering Roots and The Thornwatch Rangers. The Silverstream Keep's healers are trained, in part, by the Circle's old methods.

**Secrets:** The Circle's oldest member, who they call simply *the child*, is the same child that The Wandering Bard sang into being in the year of the Demon Wars' end. The child has not aged. The child grows new leaves every spring. The child has been waiting for someone to come home, and will not say who.

### The Hollow Hand

**Type:** Faction
 | **Location:** (480, 460)

*A cabal of merchants who trade in the things other merchants will not touch.*

The Hollow Hand is the realm's oldest and most patient secret society. They present themselves as a high-end merchant consortium, dealing in rare goods — and they are, in fact, a high-end merchant consortium, dealing in rare goods. What they are *also* is a covenant of memory-keepers, oath-brokers, and debt-collectors whose ledgers go back further than the Ironforge Clan's founding.

The Hand has no public leader. The role rotates between seven *Fingers*, each of whom serves for thirteen years and then becomes a *Palm* — an elder, an advisor, a witness. The current First Finger is unknown even to most of the Hand. The Hand's meeting place, when the Hand must meet, is The Hollow Crypt.

The Hand has been blamed, at various times, for: the Demon Wars, the Northern Kingdoms' fall, the founding of the Whispering Court, and the disappearance of King Valdris the First. None of these accusations have ever been proven. None of them have been disproven, either.

**Relationships:** Headquartered in spirit, if not always in body, at The Hollow Crypt. Holds interests in The Drowned City, The Sunken Temple, and at least three Free Caravan wagons. The Ashen Brothers have been seen dealing with the Hand, under cover, and the Hand has been seen dealing with the Black Sail Reavers, openly. The Silverstream Keep's intelligence division keeps a file on the Hand that is forty-three pages long and is missing its first page.

**Secrets:** The Hand has a *debt* — owed to it by the realm itself, in a currency the realm does not know it has. The debt was incurred in the year of the realm's founding, by an entity that no longer exists under any name the Hand will use. The debt is due. The Hand has been patient because the patient thing pays best. The patience is running out.

### The Black Sail Reavers

**Type:** Faction
 | **Location:** (730, 460)

*Sea-raiders whose flag is a black sail and whose oath is to the tide, not the throne.*

The Black Sail Reavers are the realm's most successful pirate fleet, fifty-three ships strong at last count, with bolt-holes up and down the western coast. Their base is Saltcliff Cove, but their territory is the entire western sea and any coastal road that does not have a Silverstream garrison on it.

The Reavers are not, despite their reputation, a lawless crew. They have a strict code: no slaves, no children, no killing except in self-defense or in fair challenge. They are brutal, but they are brutal by *rules*, and the rules are older than the Silverstream Keep. The rules were written by the first Reaver-Captain, Mara Blacktide, who came up from the sea one day, declared the western waters hers, and then refused to explain where she had been before.

The Reavers' arch-enemy is the Silverstream Keep's navy, but the two have an uneasy understanding. The Reavers do not raid Silverstream-flagged ships. The Silverstream navy does not pursue Reavers past the third reef. The Bridge Guild has been trying to mediate a more formal treaty for decades, with no success.

**Relationships:** Base at Saltcliff Cove, with strongholds at The Wreck of the Black Sails and The Dawnwatch Lighthouse (which they man in winter, when no one else will). Rival of The Silver Order. Have been seen dealing with The Hollow Hand — and have, in fact, *repaid* a Hand debt with the salvage from The Wreck of the Black Sails.

**Secrets:** Mara Blacktide is still alive. She is, by the most conservative count, two hundred and thirty years old. She has not aged visibly since the year of the Demon Wars' end. The Reavers do not speak of this. The Silverstream Keep's admiral has noticed. The admiral has not, yet, said anything.

## 🗺️ Locations

### Silverstream Keep

**Type:** Location
 | **Location:** (320, 180)

*The fortified heart of the Northern Pass — held by oath, garrisoned by blood, and watched by something older than either.*

Silverstream Keep is the largest inhabited fortification in the realm, a sprawl of white-grey stone and iron-banded gates built into the cliff face above the Silverstream River. Its walls have held the Northern Pass for six centuries. Its garrison, the Silver Order, is older still.

The Keep was not built to repel an army. It was built to watch a *road* — the river road that runs south from the Frostpeak passes to the lowland markets. In the year of the Demon Wars' end, Velora the Undying took the Keep from a coalition of broken northern houses and made it the Order's seat. She did not, by the chronicle, *conquer* it. She arrived at dawn, walked the walls alone, and at noon told the steward to choose: serve, or leave. Most served. The rest left before nightfall.

Today the Keep is the realm's busiest military outpost and its quietest one. Kira Dawnblade, the current Commander, has held the post at twenty-three and has not rested since. The gates are open to merchants on market days, closed to everyone else on Shadow days, and the garrison has standing orders to fire on anything that comes down the pass at a run. The pass has been too quiet this year. The garrison has been, in response, too loud.

**Relationships:** Headquarters of The Silver Order and seat of Kira Dawnblade. Garrisoned by the realm's largest standing army. Patron of The Free Caravan's midwinter passage. The Bridge Guild's western terminus. The Ironforge Clan's closest ally by treaty. Velora the Undying is the only person in living memory to have walked the walls unchallenged and left alive.

**Dangers:** The Keep's deepest cellar is older than the Keep. The Ironforge stone-masons, who raised the upper walls in the year 612, refused to work the lowest level. They sealed it with a door that no one in the Keep can open, and they left, in writing, a single instruction: *do not ask what is below; we did, and we do not speak of it.* Three Keep-Commanders, in six centuries, have asked. None of them are remembered fondly. The current Commander, Kira, has not asked. Kira is, by her own account, *saving the question*.

### Whisperwood Forest

**Type:** Location
 | **Location:** (140, 220)

*A forest that listens, and remembers, and has been listening longer than anything that lives in it has been alive.*

Whisperwood Forest is the largest unbroken woodland in the realm — a half-million acres of old-growth oak, ash, and silver-birch, threaded with paths that no map agrees on and streams that run, by local account, *uphill when the moon is dark*. The wood is not unfriendly. It is *attentive*.

The trees of the Whisperwood are, by the scholars' best guess, between four and nine thousand years old. The older ones have names. The oldest has a name no one will say aloud. The Whispering Roots, the druid faction that tends the wood, do not live in it so much as *be heard by it* — their council-meetings are held in clearings that the wood itself appears to widen or narrow as the season requires.

The wood's reputation is mixed. Hunters say their dogs will not enter it after dark. Children say the wood whispers their name back to them, exactly as they said it, three days later. The Thornwatch Rangers use the wood as a base and will not, on oath, talk about what they have seen there. The Circle of the Green has been trying, for two generations, to *listen back*. They have not, by their own report, succeeded. The wood has, by everyone's report, been listening the whole time.

**Relationships:** Tended by The Whispering Roots, the only faction the wood will permit to camp inside its borders. Patrolled by The Thornwatch Rangers, who use it as their second base. Watched, at a distance, by The Circle of the Green. The Whispering Court holds one of its three annual sittings at the wood's edge — and the wood, on those nights, goes *quieter than quiet*. Zephyrus the Oracle is the only person in living memory to have walked the wood's heart and come out the other side of it.

**Dangers:** The Whisperwood is *older than the realm*. The Stonebridge, the Keepers' flame, and the Standing Stones are all, by Hollow Hand scholarship, *younger* than the wood — built on ground the wood once owned. The wood remembers the Demon Wars. It remembers the year *before* the Demon Wars. There is a tree in the deep wood, by every account, that was already ancient when the first Keeper of the Eternal Flame took her post. The tree has a knot in its trunk that, by Zephyrus the Oracle's account, is shaped like an eye that is, by his further account, *open*.

### The Ashen Spire

**Type:** Location
 | **Location:** (650, 50)

*A blackened tower on a barren hill, lightning-struck and shunned — the seat of a fire that has never gone out, and the home of an order that has never been forgiven.*

The Ashen Spire is a tower of fused black stone on the only hill in the eastern flatlands that grows nothing. It is taller than it should be — three hundred and twelve feet, by the Bridge Guild's last survey, and the surveyors would not go higher. The hill is barren because the Spire, the scholars say, *drinks* the soil; the soil, the Spire says, is none of its concern.

The Keepers of the Eternal Flame have held the Spire since before the Demon Wars, and the flame they tend has burned the same ash-grey color for a thousand years. The flame does not consume its fuel. The flame does not flicker. The flame has been seen, by witnesses the Whispering Court considers reliable, to burn *backwards* in the wind, which is the only known instance of the fire behaving in any way its tenders did not predict.

The Ashen Brothers — the splinter order that left the Keepers in the year 642 — built their chapter-house on the far side of the hill, in a cave they call the Antechamber. The Brothers and the Keepers no longer speak. The Spire, by all accounts, *does* still speak, but only to the Keepers, and the Keepers will not repeat what it has said.

**Relationships:** Seat of the Keepers of the Eternal Flame, who have held it for a thousand years. Watched, in silence, by The Whispering Court, who consider the flame *significant*. Home, in the Antechamber, of The Ashen Brothers. The Silverstream Keep's intelligence division keeps a roster of who has gone up the Spire and who has come back. The Bridge Guild has tried, three times, to survey the upper floors and has been turned away at the door each time. Zephyrus the Oracle is one of the few non-Keepers to have entered the Spire, and the only one to have come out singing.

**Dangers:** The Ashen Spire's foundations are not *made* of stone. They are made of something older, fused by a heat that no known forge has matched since. The Hollow Hand's deepest ledger, opened only by the First Finger, contains a single entry for the Spire: *the tower is older than the hill; the hill grew up around the tower; the tower is still growing.* The current First Finger has not, by tradition, spoken of the entry. The previous First Finger went up the Spire, alone, in the year 812, and did not come down. The Keepers do not say what happened to her. The Brothers will, if pressed, *laugh*.

### The Drowned City

**Type:** Location
 | **Location:** (600, 480)

*A skeleton of a city that sank a thousand years ago, and whose topmost spires still break the surface when the tide is right.*

The Drowned City lies in the eastern bay, half a league offshore, and is visible only at the lowest tides of the year. At those times — three days in late summer, two in midwinter — the spires rise from the water like the fingers of a hand held up to keep from drowning. The city has been there, by the Keepers' count, for one thousand and forty-three years. The Bridge Guild's maps do not show it. The Silverstream Keep's naval charts show it as a *hazard*.

The city was a port, once. Its name, in the script of the time, translates roughly as *the place where the sea and the road agree*. The road, the scholars say, was a ley-line — a path of moving earth-power that ran from the Frostpeak Mountains to the southern sea. The sea and the road, the Hollow Hand claims, *disagreed*. The city fell. The topmost spires, by some mercy, did not.

The Free Caravan is *banned*, in writing, from the Drowned City. The ban is older than the current Mira. The Silverstream Keep's admiral has, three times in the last century, sent ships to *clear the hazard*. None of the ships have come back. The Hollow Hand has a *permanent delegation* in the city, by the Hand's own account — a delegation that has not, by any outside measurement, been seen to leave.

**Relationships:** Subject of an old ban by The Free Caravan, which refuses to explain the ban. The Silverstream Keep's admiralty has lost three ships to it and will not say what the ships saw.
